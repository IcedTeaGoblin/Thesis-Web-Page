Data Visualization With Ontologies
Introduction
This study is conducted by the Principal Investigator Dr. Bo Fu as part of a research project that aims to determine optimal visualizations for understanding ontology relationships in a test-based environment. This study focuses on the evaluation of two commonly used visualization techniques in their support to human users in the comprehension of mapping relationships between pairwise ontologies. This research is funded by the California State University’s Program for Education and Research in Biotechnology (CSUPERB) program.
Purpose and Objectives
The goal of this study is to assess visualization techniques and the extent to which they can support human users during interactions with ontology mappings. The findings from this research will inform the future design and development of visualization techniques for ontology mappings.

Participation Selection
No prior knowledge of ontology or ontology mappings is required. Individuals who are eighteen years old or older are invited to participate.
What is Involved?
If you consent to voluntarily participate in this research, you will be asked to complete brief tutorials on ontologies, ontology mappings, as well as visualizations for ontologies and ontology mappings. You will also be asked to perform two multiple-choice tests based on ontology mappings with accompanying visualizations. You will be asked to take part in interviews and fill out questionnaires to express your satisfaction of the given visualizations. An eye tracker will be used to observe your interaction with the given mapping visualizations and collect your gaze data. This study will take place in Room 128A in the Vivian Engineering Center (VEC), California State University of Long Beach.
